# Can an NRI File or Contest a Divorce Case in Kolkata While Living Abroad?



## Table of contents

## Introduction

**Yes, an NRI can file or defend a divorce case in Kolkata while living abroad.** Many marriages today involve one spouse residing in India and the other in the USA, UK, UAE, Canada, Australia, or another country. 

The question most people ask is: *Do I need to come back to India for divorce proceedings?* The answer is: **not always.** With proper legal planning, many stages of a divorce case in Kolkata can be managed remotely through an advocate and court-approved digital procedures.

## When Can a Divorce Case Be Filed in Kolkata?

A divorce petition may be filed in Kolkata if the Family Court has jurisdiction based on the place of marriage, the last place where the couple lived together, or the present residence of one spouse.

## Two Main Types of Divorce Cases for NRIs

### 1. Mutual Consent Divorce
If both spouses agree to separate, this is the fastest option. Key issues settled usually include alimony, child custody, and return of belongings. In suitable cases, appearance requirements may be reduced subject to court directions.

### 2. Contested Divorce
If one spouse does not agree, a contested divorce may be filed on grounds of cruelty, desertion, or adultery. These matters require stronger evidence and structured legal representation from a **divorce lawyer in Kolkata**.

## Can an NRI Contest a Divorce Case Filed in Kolkata While Abroad?

**Yes.** If your spouse has filed divorce, maintenance, or custody proceedings in Kolkata while you live abroad, you must respond immediately. Ignoring summons can result in ex-parte proceedings and adverse maintenance orders.

## Do I Need to Personally Attend Court in Kolkata?

**Not in every hearing.** Many matters may proceed through advocate appearances, video conferencing (in appropriate situations), and scheduled personal appearances only when strictly required by the court.

## Common Issues in NRI Divorce Cases

- **Maintenance / Alimony**: Claims for monthly support or a lump sum settlement.
- **Child Custody**: Visitation rights and relocation disputes.
- **Domestic Violence Cases**: Separate proceedings may be initiated alongside the divorce.
- **Foreign Divorce Decree Validity**: A foreign decree may not automatically be recognized in India.
