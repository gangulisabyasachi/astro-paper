## Introduction

If you are an NRI living in the USA, UK, UAE, Canada, Australia or any other country and have received a legal notice from Kolkata, **do not panic—but do not ignore it.**

Many NRIs receive notices related to property disputes, divorce cases, maintenance claims, or money recovery demands. A legal notice does not mean you have lost the case; it usually means a dispute has formally started. The biggest mistake people make is delay. The smartest response is quick legal review and a proper strategy.

## What Is a Legal Notice?

A legal notice is a formal communication sent by a lawyer or party before filing a case or during an ongoing dispute. It demands action, payment, reply, or compliance, and warns of legal proceedings. It is often the first serious step before litigation.

## Common Legal Notices NRIs Receive in Kolkata

NRIs commonly receive notices involving:
- **Property Disputes**: Partition, illegal possession, sale deed disputes, or tenant conflicts.
- **Divorce & Family Matters**: Divorce notice, maintenance claim, or child custody issues.
- **Money Recovery**: Loan disputes or cheque bounce complaints.
- **Civil / Commercial Matters**: Breach of contract or partnership disputes.

## What Should an NRI Do Immediately After Receiving a Notice?

### 1. Read the Notice Carefully
Check who sent it, through which lawyer, the allegations made, the documents referred to, and the time limit for a reply. Do not assume it is routine.

### 2. Preserve All Records
Keep copies of the envelope, email headers, WhatsApp messages, and any attachments. These can become important later to prove when and how you were notified.

### 3. Do Not Reply Emotionally
Avoid admissions, threats, or informal settlements without advice. Anything written may later be used against you in court.

## Do I Need to Come to India?

**In many cases, no immediate travel is required.** A large number of notice matters can be handled through:

## What Happens If I Ignore a Legal Notice?

Ignoring a genuine notice may lead to ex-parte proceedings (where the court moves forward without you), adverse interim orders, or increased litigation costs. Even if the claim is false, silence can create legally complicated situations.
