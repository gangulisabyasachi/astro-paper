**Builder Failed to Refund After Settlement? Legal Remedies Available to Homebuyers in India**

Buying a home is often the biggest financial decision in a person’s life. But what happens when a builder not only delays possession but also fails to honour a refund settlement after a consumer case is withdrawn?

This situation is becoming increasingly common—and legally, the homebuyer is not without remedies.

## The Legal Problem Explained

A typical scenario unfolds like this:

- A buyer books a flat and makes payments
- The builder fails to deliver possession on time
- The buyer files a consumer complaint
- During the case, both parties enter into a settlement agreement
- The buyer withdraws the case based on the builder’s promise to refund
- The builder then defaults on the settlement

At this stage, many buyers feel helpless. However, the law provides multiple strong remedies.

## Key Legal Issue: Breach of Settlement Agreement

A settlement agreement is legally binding.  
If the builder fails to comply, it amounts to:

✔ Breach of contract  
✔ Deficiency in service  
✔ Possible unfair trade practice  

## Legal Options Available to the Homebuyer

### 1. File a Fresh Consumer Complaint

Even if the earlier complaint was withdrawn, the buyer can:

- File a new consumer case
- Highlight non-compliance of settlement terms

Claim:

- Refund of full amount
- Interest
- Compensation for mental harassment

👉 Courts have consistently held that failure to honour settlement amounts to fresh cause of action.

### 2. Initiate Execution Proceedings (If Order Recorded Settlement)

If the settlement was:

- Recorded by the Consumer Forum, or
- Made part of a court order

Then the buyer can file an Execution Petition for enforcement.

This can lead to:

- Attachment of builder’s property
- Recovery proceedings
- Even arrest in extreme cases

### 3. File a Civil Suit for Recovery

The buyer can approach a civil court for:

- Recovery of money
- Interest
- Damages

The settlement agreement acts as strong documentary evidence.

### 4. Initiate Proceedings Under RERA

If the project is registered under RERA:

- File a complaint before the Real Estate Regulatory Authority (RERA)

Seek:

- Refund with interest
- Penalty against the builder

RERA authorities are generally homebuyer-friendly and time-bound.

### 5. Criminal Action (In Appropriate Cases)

If there is clear intention to cheat:

- A complaint under cheating (Section 420 IPC) may be filed

Applicable where:

- Builder never intended to refund
- False assurances were given to withdraw the case

⚠️ Criminal remedies should be used cautiously and with proper legal advice.

## Important Practical Advice

Before withdrawing any case based on settlement:

- Ensure written, signed agreement
- Prefer settlement recorded before court/forum
- Include clear payment timeline and penalties
- Avoid full withdrawal until substantial payment is received

A builder’s failure to honour a settlement is not the end of the road.  
In fact, it creates stronger legal grounds for action.

Homebuyers must act swiftly and strategically to enforce their rights.
