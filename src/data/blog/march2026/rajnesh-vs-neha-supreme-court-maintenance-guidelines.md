Maintenance law in India has evolved significantly over the years, but the turning point came with the Supreme Court’s landmark judgment in **Rajnesh vs Neha (2020)**. 

This decision brought much-needed clarity, consistency, and structure to maintenance proceedings, which were earlier marked by uncertainty and conflicting approaches. The case arose in the context of multiple maintenance proceedings initiated under different statutes, leading to confusion and overlapping orders. Recognizing the need for uniformity, the Supreme Court laid down comprehensive guidelines to streamline the process.

For litigants navigating matrimonial disputes, understanding this judgment is essential, whether they are seeking relief or defending a claim.

## Table of contents

## Mandatory Financial Disclosure

One of the most significant aspects of the judgment is the introduction of mandatory financial disclosure by both parties. 

The Court directed that detailed affidavits must be filed, disclosing:
* Income
* Assets
* Liabilities
* Overall financial status

This requirement has fundamentally changed the nature of maintenance cases. It ensures transparency and enables courts to make informed decisions based on actual financial capacity rather than assumptions. For any competent family lawyer in Kolkata, these affidavits now form the backbone of both claims and defences.

## Determining the Quantum of Maintenance

Another important guideline relates to the criteria for determining the quantum of maintenance. The Supreme Court emphasized that there cannot be a rigid formula. 

Instead, courts must consider multiple factors, such as:
* The standard of living during the marriage
* The reasonable needs of the claimant
* The financial obligations of the respondent

This balanced approach ensures fairness and prevents arbitrary awards. Legal professionals, including those known as the best lawyers in Kolkata for matrimonial matters, often rely on these principles to present a comprehensive picture before the court.

## Handling Multiple Maintenance Proceedings

The judgment also addressed the long-standing issue of multiple maintenance proceedings. It is now mandatory for parties to disclose any prior or pending maintenance cases. 

Courts are required to take these into account and adjust or set off amounts to avoid duplication. This has significantly reduced the scope for conflicting orders and has strengthened the defence in cases where parallel claims were previously misused.

## Date of Award and Expeditious Disposal

A notable clarification provided by the Court is regarding the date from which maintenance is to be awarded. 

* **The General Rule:** Maintenance should be granted from the **date of filing the application**, rather than the date of the order. This ensures that delays in judicial proceedings do not prejudice the claimant.
* **Timelines:** The Supreme Court further stressed the need for expeditious disposal of maintenance applications, especially interim maintenance. Delays in such matters can defeat the very purpose of providing financial support.

This underscores the importance of timely legal advice from a qualified divorce lawyer in Kolkata, particularly in cases involving financial disputes.

## Consequences of False Claims

Equally important is the Court’s stance on false or misleading claims. The requirement of financial disclosure has made it more difficult for parties to conceal income or exaggerate their needs. 

Courts are now empowered to draw adverse inferences where there is suppression of material facts. This has brought greater accountability into the system and has helped maintain the integrity of maintenance proceedings.
